# Mississauga Parking Ticket Appeal Guide

Received a parking ticket in Mississauga? Don't pay it immediately. Under Mississauga’s **Administrative Penalty System (APS)**, you have a formal right to a screening review to dispute the penalty.

## 1. Quick Facts: Mississauga Parking Rules

- **Prohibited Parking:** You cannot park on any city street between **2:00 AM and 6:00 AM** year-round unless you have a valid parking permit.

- **Time Limits:** The standard limit for on-street parking is **5 hours** unless otherwise posted.

- **Fire Hydrants:** You must maintain a distance of **3 metres** from any fire hydrant.

## 2. Common Parking Ticket Hotspots in Mississauga

- **Celebration Square / City Centre:** High enforcement during events and around Square One Shopping Centre.

- **Port Credit:** Busy waterfront area with strict seasonal enforcement and limited pay-and-display zones.

- **Streetsville:** Popular dining destination where time-limit violations are frequently issued.

- **Mississauga Hospital (Trillium):** Heavy enforcement in surrounding residential streets and hospital-managed lots.

## 3. The 3-Step "Deep Strike" Defense (Appeal to Court) Your Mississauga Ticket

In Mississauga, disputes are handled through a **Screening Officer**. You must request a review within **15 days** of the ticket being issued.

### Step-by-Step Appeal Process:

1. **Visit the Portal:** Go to the [Mississauga Pay or Dispute Portal]({{portal_url}}).

2. **Enter Details:** Provide your ticket number and vehicle license plate.

3. **Choose 'Dispute':** Select the option to request a **Screening Review**.

4. **Submit Evidence:** Upload photos of signs, your parking permit, or proof of a broken meter.

5. **Wait for Result:** A Screening Officer will review your case and may cancel, reduce, or uphold the penalty.

## 4. Valid Grounds for Appeal

| Issue | Recommended Defense |

|---|---|

| **Broken Meter** | Provide the meter number and a photo of the error message or "Out of Order" sign. |

| **Emergency** | Provide documented proof of a medical or mechanical emergency (tow receipts, hospital records). |

| **Incorrect Plate/Make** | If the officer recorded the wrong vehicle information, the ticket may be technically invalid. |

| **Conflicting Signs** | Provide clear photos showing that the signs were blocked, missing, or contradictory. |

## 5. What Happens After the Screening?

If you are unhappy with the Screening Officer's decision, you can request an additional review by a **Hearing Officer**. Note that Hearing Officer decisions are final and binding.
