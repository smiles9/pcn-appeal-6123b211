## Appealing Impark Notices in Canada

**Impark (now part of Reef)** is one of the largest parking operators in North America. If you park in a private lot in **Toronto, Vancouver, or Calgary**, chances are it's managed by Impark. 

If you find an Impark "Notice" on your car, don't panic. Understanding Canadian civil law is your best defense.

## The Difference Between 'Fines' and 'Invoices'

In Canada, there is a strict legal distinction between a **Municipality Fine** (issued by the city) and a **Private Invoice** (issued by a company).

- **City Fines**: These are backed by bylaws. If you don't pay, you cannot renew your license or registration.
- **Impark Invoices**: These are private claims. They have **no authority** over your driver's license or vehicle registration in most provinces (including Ontario, BC, and Alberta).

## Winning Grounds for Canadian Appeals

### 1. The 'Signage' Defense
For a contract to be valid, the terms must be clearly visible *before* you enter the lot. If the signs are only located at the back of the lot or are obscured by snow or other vehicles, you can argue that no contract was formed.

### 2. Penalty vs. Damages
Under Canadian law, a private company can only claim for **damages** (the revenue they lost). If you stayed 15 minutes late and they charge you $90, this is legally considered a "Penalty." In Canadian civil law, penalty clauses in contracts are often unenforceable.

### 3. Proof of Landowner Authority
The parking company must have the legal right to sue you on behalf of the property owner. If they cannot prove they have a valid contract with the owner of the land, their claim fails.

## The Canadian Appeal Process

### Step 1: Online Dispute
Visit the Impark/Reef dispute portal. 
- **Be technical**: State that "the amount claimed is an unenforceable penalty and does not represent a genuine pre-estimate of loss."
- **Evidence**: Upload photos of any missing or confusing signs.

### Step 2: Dealing with Collections
If you don't pay, you will receive letters from a collection agency (often "Wiggin and Dana" or similar). 
- **Fact**: They cannot put a 'hit' on your credit report for a private parking debt without a court order.
- **Action**: You can send a "cease and desist" letter requiring them to only contact you through the courts.

## Should You Pay?
Many Canadians choose to pay to avoid the annoyance of collection letters. However, if the charge is excessive or the signage was poor, you have every right to dispute it.
