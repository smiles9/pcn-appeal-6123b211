## Fighting a Parking Fine in Darwin

**The City of Darwin and the Darwin Waterfront Corporation actively enforce parking rules to ensure turnover in the CBD and popular tourist areas.** If you've received a 'yellow envelope' on your windscreen, you have the right to request a review if you believe the fine was issued unfairly or in error.

This guide provides the specific steps and legal context for appealing a parking infringement in the Northern Territory's capital.

## The Appeal Process: Step-by-Step

### 1. Internal Review (City of Darwin)

The first step is always an internal review. You should not pay the fine if you intend to appeal, as payment is often considered an admission of liability.

*   **Online:** Use the City of Darwin's 'Apply for an Infringement Review' portal.

*   **Email:** Send your appeal to `darwin@darwin.nt.gov.au`.

*   **Post:** Mail your letter to GPO Box 84, Darwin, NT 0801.

**What to include:**

*   The Infringement Number.

*   Your vehicle registration.

*   A clear explanation of why you are contesting the fine.

*   Supporting evidence (photos of signs, medical certificates, mechanic invoices).

### 2. Statutory Declarations

If you sold the vehicle before the offence or another person was driving, you cannot simply write a letter. You must submit a **Statutory Declaration** naming the responsible party. This is a legal document that must be witnessed by a Commissioner for Oaths, Justice of the Peace, or other authorized person.

### 3. Darwin Waterfront Special Rules

If your ticket was issued at the Darwin Waterfront (managed by the Waterfront Corporation), be aware they are notably stricter. They specifically state that "the ticket fell down" or "I used the wrong zone on the app" are **not** valid grounds for appeal.

## Common Grounds for Success in Darwin

While "I was only five minutes late" rarely works, the following situations often result in a withdrawal:

1.  **Medical Emergencies:** If you or a passenger required urgent medical attention. You must provide a letter from the hospital or doctor.

2.  **Mechanical Breakdown:** If the vehicle could not be moved. Provide a receipt from a towing company or a roadside assistance report (e.g., AANT).

3.  **Signage Issues:** If signs were missing, obscured by foliage, or contradicted each other. Photographic evidence is essential here.

4.  **Meter/Equipment Failure:** If the parking meter was faulty and you reported it at the time.

## What Happens Next?

Once you lodge an appeal, the fine is suspended. The City of Darwin will investigate and notify you of the outcome via mail or email.

*   **If Successful:** The infringement is withdrawn, and no further action is required.

*   **If Unsuccessful:** You will be given a new due date to pay the original amount. No extra fees are added for the time taken to review.

*   **Final Option:** If you still disagree, you can elect to go to court. This is a significant step and usually only recommended if you have strong legal grounds or evidence the council has ignored.

## Darwin Parking Tips

*   **Use the Apps:** PayStay and EasyPark are widely used in Darwin. Ensure you select the correct zone code (printed on the meter).

*   **Check the Signs:** Darwin has many 'Loading Zones' and 'Permit Only' areas that are strictly enforced, especially during business hours (8:00 AM to 5:00 PM).

*   **Motorcycles:** Usually have designated free parking areas in the CBD—check the City of Darwin website for a map of these spots to avoid fines.
