## How AI Diagnoses Your Parking Ticket

The process behind an AI diagnosis is more complex than it looks on the surface. Here is the step-by-step breakdown:

### 1. OCR (Optical Character Recognition)

When you upload a photo of your PCN, the AI uses OCR to extract key data:

- **PCN Number** (identifies the issuing authority)

- **Contravention Code** (e.g., Code 01: Restricted Street)

- **Date and Time** (checks for "14-day" and "28-day" rule compliance)

- **Location** (allows the AI to look up specific local bylaws)

### 2. Legal Cross-Referencing

Once the AI knows the *reason* for the ticket, it cross-references it with:

- **TMA 2004** (Traffic Management Act) for council fines.

- **PoFA 2012** (Protection of Freedoms Act) for private charges.

- **TSRGD 2016** (Traffic Signs Regulations and General Directions) for signage and marking errors.

- **Local TROs** (Traffic Regulation Orders) — the local laws that actually make the yellow lines legal.

### 3. Vulnerability Identification

The AI looks for "weak points" that a human might miss:

- **Missing Information**: Did the council forget to include the mandatory "Right to Appeal" wording?

- **Invalid Timelines**: Was the Notice to Keeper sent too late?

- **Ambiguous Location**: Is the location description on the PCN too vague to be legally enforceable?
