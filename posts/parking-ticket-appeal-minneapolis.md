# How to Appeal a Parking Ticket in Minneapolis

If you’ve received a parking citation in Minneapolis, you have the right to contest it through an administrative hearing process. Most disputes can be handled online or by mail without needing a court appearance.

## Minneapolis Parking Ticket Hotspots
Enforcement is particularly strict in high-traffic areas. Watch out for these landmarks:
*   **Target Center / Target Field:** Heavy enforcement during games and concerts. "Event Parking" rules apply and are strictly enforced.
*   **University of Minnesota (UMN):** Areas around East and West Bank campuses are frequently ticketed for expired meters and permit violations.
*   **North Loop:** A trending area with complex parking rules; "No Parking" during specific hours is a common trap.
*   **Dinkytown:** High turnover and frequent ticketing for exceeding time limits.

## How to Dispute Your Ticket
In Minneapolis, you can request an **Administrative Review** if you believe the ticket was issued in error.

1.  **Online Appeal:** Use the [Minneapolis Online Appeal Form](https://www.minneapolismn.gov/government/departments/reg-services/administrative-hearings/appeals/online/) to submit your case.
2.  **Required Info:** You will need your citation number and vehicle plate number.
3.  **Evidence:** Upload clear photos of any signage you believe is confusing, a photo of a broken meter screen, or proof of a valid permit/payment.
4.  **Timeline:** It is best to appeal within **30 days** of the citation date to avoid late fees.

## Common Successful Grounds for Appeal
| Ground | Documentation Needed |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Broken Meter** | Meter number and photo of the display/app error message. |
| **Snow Emergency** | Proof that the car was moved before the deadline or that signs were not visible. |
| **Permit Holder** | Photo of your valid parking permit and its placement in the car. |

## FAQ
|A| Can I ignore a Minneapolis parking ticket? ||| No. Unpaid tickets will lead to late fees, potential towing ("The Boot"), and can prevent registration renewal.
|A| How long does an online appeal take? ||| Most reviews are completed within 7 to 14 business days.
|A| What if my appeal is denied? ||| You can request a formal hearing with an Administrative Hearing Officer for a secondary review.
