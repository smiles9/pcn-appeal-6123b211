# Bristol Parking Ticket (PCN) Appeal Guide (2026)

Bristol is known for its strict parking enforcement, particularly within its Clean Air Zone (CAZ) and busy shopping districts. Whether you've been ticketed on-street or in a council-run car park, understanding Bristol's specific observation rules and hotspots is key to a successful appeal.

## Bristol Parking Hotspots (2026 Alert)

Bristol City Council's enforcement teams are particularly active in the following areas:

- **Gloucester Road (BS7)**: Frequent ticketing for loading bay violations and double yellow line infringements during peak hours.

- **Whiteladies Road (BS8)**: High enforcement of "Pay & Display" expiry and permit-only bays.

- **Temple Meads / Central Zone (BS1)**: Strict monitoring of "No Loading" restrictions and short-stay bays near the station.

- **Baldwin Street / Bristol Bridge**: While primarily bus lane enforcement, many drivers receive PCNs here for accidental entry into restricted zones.

## Winning Appeal Grounds in Bristol

### 1. The 10-Minute Grace Period

In accordance with the **Deregulation Act 2015**, Bristol City Council must grant a 10-minute grace period after your parking time has expired. If your PCN was issued within 10 minutes of your ticket or permit expiry in a lawful bay, it is invalid.

### 2. Inadequate Signage (TSRGD 2016)

Signage must be clear and compliant with the **Traffic Signs Regulations and General Directions (TSRGD) 2016**. If a sign was obscured by foliage, vandalized, or placed too far from the bay, your appeal has a high chance of success.

### 3. Procedural Impropriety

The PCN itself must contain specific information, including the reason for the fine, the amount, and how to appeal. If any mandatory information is missing, the notice is unenforceable.

## How to Submit Your Bristol Appeal

### Online Portal

The fastest way to appeal is via the [Bristol City Council PCN Portal](https://www.bristol.gov.uk/residents/parking/parking-fines-bus-lane-fines-and-towed-away/appeal-a-parking-or-bus-lane-fine). You will need your PCN number (beginning with 'OB' or 'BS') and your vehicle registration.

### Postal Appeal

Address your formal challenge to:

**Parking Services (PCN Appeals), PO Box 3399, Bristol City Council, Bristol, BS1 9NE.**

## The "Shotgun" Strategy

Don't just provide one reason. If the signage was poor AND you were within the grace period, state both. Bristol's adjudicators are more likely to cancel a ticket when faced with multiple valid legal points.
