## Parking Tickets in New York City: The Essentials

**New York City issues millions of parking tickets each year, generating nearly $1 billion in revenue.** From alternate side parking to fire hydrants and double parking, the city's enforcement is aggressive. However, NYC has a formal adjudication process that allows you to dispute any ticket you believe was issued in error.

## Common NYC Parking Violations and Defenses

### Alternate Side Parking (ASP)

NYC's most common violation. You must move your car during specific times for street cleaning.

- **Defense**: If the street was not cleaned or if the signs were missing/unreadable.

- **ASP Suspension**: Check the @NYCASP Twitter/X account or NYC.gov to see if ASP was suspended on the day you were ticketed.

### Fire Hydrant (VC 40)

You must park at least 15 feet away from a fire hydrant.

- **Defense**: Prove with photos and a measuring tape that your car was more than 15 feet away. NYC judges are very strict about this measurement.

### No Standing/No Parking

"No Standing" means you can't wait in your car, while "No Parking" allows you to stop briefly to load/unload.

- **Defense**: "Broken meter" is no longer a valid defense in most cases. Focus on unclear signage or if you were actively loading/unloading where permitted.

## How to Dispute an NYC Parking Ticket

### Step 1: Request a Hearing

You have **30 days** from the ticket date to request a hearing.

- **Online**: Use the **NYC.gov/Finance** portal or the **NYC Pay or Dispute** app. This is the fastest method.

- **By Mail**: Send your ticket and evidence to:

*NYC Department of Finance, Hearing-by-Mail Unit, P.O. Box 29021, Brooklyn, NY 11202-9021*.

- **In Person**: Visit a **Finance Business Center** in any of the five boroughs (no appointment usually necessary).

### Step 2: Present Your Evidence

An **Administrative Law Judge (ALJ)** will review your case.

- **Photos**: Take clear photos showing your car, the ticket on the windshield, and the surrounding signs.

- **Evidence**: Include a copy of your registration, a map of the location, or a witness statement.

- **Decision**: You will receive a decision immediately (in person) or within 3-4 weeks (online/mail).

### Step 3: The Appeals Board

If the ALJ finds you guilty, you can appeal.

- You have **30 days** from the date of the hearing decision to file an appeal.

- You must pay the fine and any penalties before you can appeal (they will be refunded if you win).

- **Success Rate**: Only about **15%** of appeals to the Board are successful. You must prove the original judge made a mistake of law or fact.

## Camera Violations in NYC

NYC also issues tickets for:

- **Red Light Cameras**

- **School Zone Speed Cameras**

- **Bus Lane Cameras**

These are $50 fines and do not result in points on your license. They can be disputed through the same process as parking tickets.

## NYC Parking Ticket Checklist

- [ ] **Check for Errors**: Does the ticket have the correct plate number, state, and vehicle make? Any "required element" error can lead to an automatic dismissal.

- [ ] **Take Photos**: Always take photos of the signs and your car's position relative to the curb or hydrant.

- [ ] **Check ASP Status**: Was street cleaning actually scheduled for that day?

- [ ] **Submit Early**: Don't wait until the 30th day. Submit your dispute as soon as possible to avoid late fees.

- [ ] **Keep Records**: Save your hearing request confirmation and any evidence you submitted.
