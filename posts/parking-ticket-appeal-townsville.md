## Fighting a Parking Fine in Townsville

**Parking enforcement in Townsville is managed by the Townsville City Council under the *Transport Operations (Road Use Management) Act 1995*.** Whether you were fined in the CBD, near The Strand, or around James Cook University, the process to contest it is consistent across the city.

This guide outlines how to navigate the Queensland appeals system and the specific local factors that can help your case in Townsville.

## The Appeal Process: Townsville City Council

In Townsville, the first step is always an **Internal Review**. This is a request for the council to reconsider the fine based on the evidence you provide.

### 1. Request an Internal Review

The most effective way to appeal is online via the **Townsville City Council's 'Request a Parking Fine Review' portal**.

**What to include in your appeal:**

*   **The Infringement Number:** Found on the top of your notice.

*   **Your vehicle registration:** Must match the fine.

*   **A clear explanation:** Why the fine should be withdrawn (e.g., "The signage was obscured by a large tree branch").

*   **Supporting Evidence:** Photos of the scene, GPS logs, medical certificates, or towing receipts.

*   **A Statutory Declaration:** If you were not the driver or were testifying as a witness.

### 2. SPER (State Penalties Enforcement Registry)

If you do not pay or appeal the fine within 28 days, it may be referred to **SPER**. SPER can add significant enforcement costs and has the power to suspend your driver's license or seize property. It is essential to act early before the matter reaches SPER.

### 3. Magistrates' Court Election

If your internal review is rejected, you have the right to **elect to go to court**. This moves the matter to the Townsville Magistrates' Court on Old Cleveland Road. Only take this step if you have strong evidence that the fine was issued unlawfully, as the court can impose higher fines and legal costs.

## Common Grounds for Success in Townsville

The following situations often result in a withdrawal or a caution in Townsville:

1.  **Obscured or Faulty Signage:** In areas like Flinders Street or The Strand, signs can sometimes be obscured by palm trees or tropical foliage. If you can prove the sign was not clearly visible, the fine is often withdrawn.

2.  **Vehicle Breakdown:** If your car suffered a mechanical failure (e.g., flat battery, engine failure in the heat). You must provide a receipt from a mechanic or a roadside assistance provider like the RACQ.

3.  **Medical Emergency:** If you had to park urgently to attend to a medical crisis at Townsville University Hospital or a local clinic. A letter from a doctor or hospital is required.

4.  **Incorrect Vehicle Details:** If the officer recorded the wrong registration, make, or model of your car, the fine may be invalid.

## Townsville Specifics: High-Enforcement Zones

*   **Townsville CBD (Flinders St, Sturt St):** High enforcement of 'No Stopping' and 'Loading Zones' during business hours (8:00 AM to 5:00 PM).

*   **The Strand:** Rangers are extremely active here, especially on weekends and public holidays. Check for 'No Parking' signs near the water.

*   **Ferry Terminal (Breakwater Terminal):** Parking around the terminal for Magnetic Island is strictly monitored. Ensure you have paid for the correct duration.

## What Happens Next?

Once you lodge your review, the Townsville City Council will place the fine **on hold**. You will receive a letter or email with one of three outcomes:

1.  **Penalty Withdrawn:** No fine to pay, no record of the offence.

2.  **Caution Issued:** The offence is recorded, but you don't have to pay the fine. This is common for minor first-time offences with valid excuses.

3.  **Penalty Confirmed:** You must pay the fine by the new due date or elect to go to court.
