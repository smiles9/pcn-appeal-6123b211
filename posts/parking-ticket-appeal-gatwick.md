# Gatwick Airport PCN Appeal Guide

Gatwick Airport (LGW) operates a strict drop-off charge system. If you fail to pay the £6 charge by midnight the following day, you will receive a £100 PCN (Parking Charge Notice) from NCP.

## 1. The Gatwick Drop-off Rule
The charge is compulsory for anyone entering the terminal drop-off zones. Automated Number Plate Recognition (ANPR) cameras track every vehicle. 
*   **Cost:** £6 for up to 10 minutes.
*   **Late Payment:** Not possible. If you miss the midnight deadline, the PCN is triggered automatically.

## 2. High-Enforcement Areas
*   **North Terminal Drop-off:** Cameras are positioned at both entrance and exit.
*   **South Terminal Drop-off:** High congestion often leads to "overstaying" the 10-minute window.
*   **Red Routes:** Strictly no stopping anywhere on airport approach roads.

## 3. How to Appeal an NCP Gatwick PCN
1.  **Do Not Pay:** Paying is seen as an admission of liability.
2.  **Locate Your PCN:** You will need the reference number (usually starting with 'CP').
3.  **Online Portal:** Go to the [NCP Appeal Portal](https://www.ncp.co.uk/help-centre/gatwick-airport/) or follow the link on the Gatwick transport page.
4.  **Grounds for Appeal:**
    *   **Blue Badge Holder:** Blue badge holders are exempt but *must* register their vehicle. If you forgot, submit a photo of your badge.
    *   **Payment Failure:** If the payment website was down, provide a screenshot or timestamped proof.
    *   **ANPR Error:** ANPR cameras sometimes fail to record an exit, making it look like a 24-hour stay.

## 4. POFA Schedule 4 Defense
Gatwick is governed by Airport Byelaws, meaning it is not "relevant land" under the Protection of Freedoms Act 2012 (POFA). NCP often fails to word their notices correctly to transfer liability from the driver to the keeper.
