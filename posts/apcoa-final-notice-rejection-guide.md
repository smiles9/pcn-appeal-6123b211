# APCOA Final Notice Rejection: The Survival Guide

If you've received a letter from **APCOA** titled **"Final Notice"** or a formal rejection of your initial appeal, you are entering the most critical phase of the parking ticket lifecycle.

**Do not panic.** Most APCOA rejections are generated by automated systems and do not account for the specific legal nuances of UK parking law, especially regarding Airport Byelaws.

## 1. The POPLA Code (Your Golden Ticket)

When APCOA rejects a formal appeal, they are legally required to provide you with a **10-digit verification code** for **POPLA (Parking on Private Land Appeals)**.

- **Deadline**: You have 28 days from the date of the rejection letter to use this code.

- **The Trap**: If they haven't provided a code, they are in breach of the British Parking Association (BPA) Code of Practice. You can report them to the BPA immediately.

## 2. Why APCOA Rejects Your Appeal (The Standard Script)

APCOA almost always uses the following reasons:

- *"The signage was clear and visible."*

- *"The 24-hour payment window was not met."*

- *"Technical failures of the app are the responsibility of the driver."*

**The Counter-Strike**: None of these reasons address the **"No Keeper Liability"** issue. If the ticket was issued on Airport land (Heathrow, Manchester, Stansted), the Protection of Freedoms Act 2012 (POFA) does not apply. If you haven't named the driver, APCOA has no legal path to hold the keeper responsible.

## 3. Dealing with Debt Recovery Plus (DRP)

If you've reached the "Final Notice" stage, APCOA may have already passed your details to **Debt Recovery Plus (DRP)**.

- **Fact**: DRP are not bailiffs. They have NO power to enter your home or seize goods.

- **The Strategy**: DRP letters are designed to create 'fear of court.' However, most airport cases are dropped once a robust POPLA appeal or a "Statutory Breach" notice is sent.

## 4. Next Steps: The POPLA Submission

Your POPLA appeal should focus on three specific points:

1.  **Non-Relevant Land**: Citing that the land is governed by Byelaws and therefore POFA Schedule 4 cannot be used to transfer liability to the keeper.

2.  **Lack of Standing**: Challenging whether APCOA actually has the written authority from the landowner to issue court claims.

3.  **Grace Period**: Citing the mandatory BPA 5-10 minute grace period for the driver to read the terms.
