## Appealing Wilson Parking Notices in Australia

**Wilson Parking is the largest private parking operator in Australia.** If you've received a "Payment Notice" on your windscreen in Sydney, Melbourne, or Perth, you're not alone. Thousands of these notices are issued daily. 

However, it is vital to understand that **these are not government fines.** They are invoices for an alleged breach of contract.

## The Legal Reality in Australia

In Australia, only statutory bodies have the power to issue "Penalty Notices." Private companies like Wilson and **Secure Parking** rely on **Contract Law**. 

1. **The Contract**: When you drive into a private car park, you are deemed to have accepted the terms and conditions displayed on the signs.
2. **The Breach**: If you overstay or park incorrectly, they claim you breached that contract.
3. **The Remedy**: They can only claim "liquidated damages"—which means the actual money they lost because of your breach (usually just the parking fee plus a small admin cost).

**Claiming $80 for a $5 parking overstay is often considered an "unlawful penalty" rather than a genuine loss.**

## Top Grounds for Appeal (AU)

### 1. Inadequate or Misleading Signage
Under the **Australian Consumer Law (ACL)**, signs must be clear, visible, and not misleading. If the terms are in tiny print or hidden behind a pillar, the contract is likely unenforceable.

### 2. Unfair Contract Terms
The ACL protects consumers against "unfair contract terms." A term that imposes a large financial penalty for a small breach (like being 5 minutes late) can be challenged as unfair and void.

### 3. No Genuine Pre-estimate of Loss
If the operator claims $88 for a breach that cost them $4 in lost revenue, the charge is a penalty. In Australian law, penalties in contracts are generally unenforceable.

## The AU Appeal Process

### Stage 1: The Internal Appeal
Write to Wilson Parking's "Appeals" department. 
- Use terms like "Liquidated Damages" and "Genuine Pre-estimate of Loss."
- Mention the "Australian Consumer Law" and "Unfair Contract Terms."
- Do not admit to being the driver; refer to yourself as the "Registered Owner."

### Stage 2: Debt Collection & Court
If they reject your appeal, they will likely hand the "debt" to a collection agency. These agencies have no more power than the parking company itself. They cannot "blacklist" your credit score without a court judgment.

## Should You Pay?
Most legal experts suggest that if you have a valid defense (like bad signs or an unfair charge amount), you should appeal. Paying is an admission of the debt.
