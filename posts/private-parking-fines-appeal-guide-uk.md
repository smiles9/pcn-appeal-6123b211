Private parking companies like UKPC, ParkingEye, and Horizon often issue "Parking Charge Notices." Note the subtle difference: these are technically invoices for a supposed breach of contract, not criminal fines.

## The Secret to Beating Private Fines

### 1. The BPA/IPC Code of Practice
Most major firms belong to either the British Parking Association (BPA) or the International Parking Community (IPC). If they violate their own code of practice (e.g., by not providing a 10-minute grace period), the charge is often unenforceable.

### 2. 'Fairness' and Overcharging
The landmark 'Beavis' case set rules for how much private firms can charge. If the amount is punitive and doesn't represent a "genuine pre-estimate of loss" (and doesn't follow strict Supreme Court guidelines), it can be challenged.

### 3. Signage Visibility
If the entrance sign doesn't clearly state the price in a large font, there is no "contract" and you don't have to pay.

## POPLA vs IAS
If your initial appeal to the company fails, you have the right to an independent appeal through **POPLA** (for BPA members) or **IAS** (for IPC members). Our AI tool specializes in writing the specific multi-page documents required for these tribunals.
