# How to Appeal a Parking Ticket in Calgary (2026 Guide)

Calgary's parking laws are strictly enforced by Calgary Parking (part of the City's Business Operations). However, if you've received a ticket at the **Core Mall**, **Fort Calgary**, or **Mount Royal**, you might have a strong case for an appeal.

## The Calgary Dispute Process

Calgary uses a two-stage Administrative Penalty System (APS) that is designed to keep parking disputes out of traditional traffic court.

1. **Stage 1: Calgary Parking Administrative Review**

- You must request an Administrative Review (AR) within 30 days of the ticket being issued.

- You can submit your appeal online through the [Calgary Parking portal](https://www.calgaryparking.com/tickets/appeal.html).

- An independent review officer will evaluate your request and the evidence (including photos taken by the officer).

2. **Stage 2: Independent Hearing (Hearing Officer)**

- If the Administrative Review is unsuccessful, you have a further 15 days to request a Hearing.

- A Hearing Officer will review the decision and evidence to ensure the law was applied correctly.

## High-Ticket Hotspots in Calgary

Calgary's parking enforcement "sharks" are particularly active in several areas:

- **The Core Mall & 7th Avenue:** Loading zone violations and expired meters in the downtown core are the most frequent tickets issued.

- **Fort Calgary & Inglewood:** Special event parking and residential-only permit zones are highly monitored.

- **SAIT & Southern Alberta Jubilee Auditorium:** Student and event parking is strictly enforced, especially during peak hours.

- **Municipal Impound Lot (400 39th Ave SE):** If your car is towed, it will be taken here, and you will face additional fees on top of the ticket.
