---# Beating a Gloucester Council Parking Ticket: The 2026 Strategy

Receiving a Penalty Charge Notice (PCN) in Gloucester—whether you were visiting the historic Docks or shopping at the Quays—can be frustrating. However, in 2026, Gloucestershire County Council is required to follow strict enforcement codes. If they haven't followed the rules, you don't have to pay.

## Core Appeal Grounds for Gloucester
Gloucester uses a mix of static ANPR and on-foot Civil Enforcement Officers (CEOs). Most successful appeals in Gloucester center on:
1. **The 10-Minute Grace Period**: Under UK law, you have a 10-minute grace period *after* your paid parking expires in a council-run bay. If you were ticketed at minute 8, the PCN is invalid.
2. **Faded Road Markings**: In areas like Eastgate Street, heavy traffic often wears down yellow lines. If the T-bar is missing or the line is broken, it is legally unenforceable.
3. **Observation Time Failures**: CEOs must observe a vehicle for a set period (usually 5 minutes) to ensure no loading/unloading is taking place. If the PCN was issued instantly, you have a case.

## Where to Appeal
You should submit your challenge online via the [Gloucestershire County Council Parking Portal](https://www.gloucestershire.gov.uk/parking/). Do not pay the fine if you intend to appeal, as payment is considered an admission of guilt.
