## Parking Tickets in Nice: What You Need to Know

**Nice — the capital of the French Riviera (Côte d'Azur) and France's fifth-largest city — has parking enforcement that intensifies dramatically during the tourist season from May to October.** Métropole Nice Côte d'Azur issues the FPS (Forfait de Post-Stationnement) at €30, and enforcement agents patrol the Promenade des Anglais, Vieille Ville, and port areas with particular attention.

Nice follows the national Code de la route and the 2018 FPS system, but its status as one of Europe's premier tourist destinations drives seasonal enforcement patterns unlike most other French cities.

> **Key stat**: Nice receives over 5 million visitors annually, and parking enforcement revenue increases substantially during the May-October tourist season as demand for limited parking spaces surges.

## High-Enforcement Areas in Nice

### Promenade des Anglais

**The Promenade des Anglais, Nice's iconic seafront boulevard, has limited street parking and intense enforcement.** The seaward side has virtually no public parking (reserved for taxis and delivery). The parallel streets (Rue de France, Rue Meyerbeer, Rue de la Buffa) have metered parking but fill quickly during summer. Enforcement patrols continuously from 9:00 to 19:00 (extending to 21:00 in summer months). The Parking Massena and Parking Albert 1er underground car parks are more reliable options.

### Vieille Ville (Old Town)

Nice's Vieille Ville around Cours Saleya, the flower market, and Place Rossetti is fully pedestrianised. No public parking exists within the Old Town. Vehicle entry is restricted to residents (with permits) and delivery vehicles (early morning only). The Parking Saleya (underground beneath Cours Saleya) provides direct access to the Old Town. The Parking du Château near the Colline du Château provides another option.

### Port Area

The Port de Nice (Port Lympia) area has metered parking along the Quai des Docks and surrounding streets. During cruise ship arrivals and ferry sailings (Corsica Ferries to Corsica and Sardinia), parking demand spikes and enforcement increases. The Boulevard Stalingrad and Place de l'Île de Beauté area has resident-priority zones.

### Cimiez

The Cimiez hill district, home to the Matisse Museum and Roman ruins, has limited street parking with resident zones. Visitors to the museums should use the small car park on Avenue des Arènes de Cimiez. During the Jazz à Nice festival (July), temporary restrictions apply throughout the area.

## How to Appeal a Nice Parking Ticket (FPS)

### Step 1: Identify the Ticket Type

Confirm whether it is an FPS (€30, marked "Forfait de Post-Stationnement") or a traffic fine (amende, up to €135, marked "Avis de Contravention"). The appeal processes are different.

### Step 2: File a RAPO

Submit a **RAPO** within **one month** of the FPS date:

- **Online** via antai.gouv.fr

- **By post** to the address on the FPS

Include the FPS reference, carte grise details, grounds, and evidence.

### Step 3: CCSP Appeal

If rejected, appeal to the **CCSP** in Limoges within **one month**. Pay the FPS first; refund if successful.

## Strong Grounds for Appeal in Nice

1. **Wrong vehicle identification** — Check the plate on the FPS carefully. Nice has a high proportion of foreign-registered vehicles (Italian, Monegasque, British), increasing the chance of plate-reading errors.

2. **Valid payment via app** — If you paid via PayByPhone but the system did not register, provide timestamped payment confirmation.

3. **Meter malfunction** — Nice's coastal environment (salt air, heat) degrades horodateurs. Document any malfunction with photos.

4. **Vehicle sold or stolen** — Provide certificat de cession or police report.

5. **Seasonal restriction confusion** — Some Nice zones have different enforcement hours in summer vs. winter. If the horodateur signage was unclear about seasonal hours, document this.

## Nice-Specific Legal Points

1. **Carnaval de Nice**: During the Carnaval de Nice (February), extensive street closures and temporary parking restrictions affect the area around Place Masséna and the Promenade du Paillon. Temporary signs are posted, and vehicles in violation are towed.

2. **Event security zones**: Following security concerns, the Promenade des Anglais has permanent bollards and restricted vehicle access at certain points. Parking near security barriers is prohibited and strictly enforced.

3. **Cross-border considerations**: Nice's proximity to the Italian border means many Italian-registered vehicles receive FPS tickets. Italian drivers can appeal through the standard RAPO process. Cross-border enforcement between France and Italy applies for unpaid fines.
