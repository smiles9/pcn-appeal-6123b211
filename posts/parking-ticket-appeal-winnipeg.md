# How to Appeal a Parking Ticket in Winnipeg (2026 Guide)

The Winnipeg Parking Authority (WPA) is known for its efficient enforcement, but many drivers aren't aware of their right to a "Screening Review." If you believe your ticket was issued in error, follow this guide to challenge it.

## The Winnipeg Appeal Process

Winnipeg uses the *Municipal By-law Enforcement Act* to handle parking disputes through an administrative process.

1. **Request a Screening Review**

- You must request a review within 30 days of the ticket date.

- You can submit your request online at [The Parking Store (City of Winnipeg)](https://theparkingstore.winnipeg.ca/).

- A Screening Officer will independently review the ticket and any evidence you provide.

2. **Adjudication (Hearing)**

- If the Screening Officer upholds the ticket, you can pay the fine or request a hearing before a Provincially-appointed Adjudicator.

- A $25 adjudication fee is charged if the Adjudicator finds that the ticket was valid.

## High-Ticket Hotspots in Winnipeg

Certain areas in Winnipeg are "gold mines" for parking enforcement:

- **Canada Life Centre (Downtown):** During Jets games or concerts, "No Parking" and event-specific zones are strictly enforced. New Taxi/Rideshare zones implemented in Jan 2026 are a common point of confusion.

- **Burton Cummings Theatre:** Narrow streets and strict loading zone rules lead to frequent tickets during show times.

- **University of Manitoba (Fort Garry):** The University issues its own citations which are valid and can lead to academic holds if unpaid.

- **Exchange District:** Popular for dining but has limited street parking and complex loading zone schedules.
