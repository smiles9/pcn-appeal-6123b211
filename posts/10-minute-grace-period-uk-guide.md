Did you know that in the UK, many parking tickets are issued illegally within the first 10 minutes of your parking session ending? 

Whether you are dealing with a local council PCN or a private parking firm like UKPC or ParkingEye, the **10-minute grace period** is one of the strongest legal tools in your arsenal. In this guide, we break down exactly how it works and how to use it to win your appeal.

## 1. What is the 10-Minute Grace Period?

Since April 2015, the UK government has mandated that local councils in England must allow a 10-minute grace period before issuing a Penalty Charge Notice (PCN). This applies to:
- On-street parking bays.
- Council-run car parks.

**Private Parking Companies**: While the law is slightly different for private land, the British Parking Association (BPA) and the International Parking Community (IPC) code of practice also mandate a minimum 10-minute grace period for their members.

## 2. When Does the 10 Minutes Start?

The 10-minute window begins the moment your paid-for parking time expires. 
*Example: If your ticket expires at 2:00 PM, you cannot be issued a ticket until 2:11 PM.*

If the timestamp on your ticket shows it was issued at 2:05 PM, that ticket is likely **invalid and should be cancelled immediately upon appeal.**

## 3. The 3-Step "Deep Strike" Defense (Appeal to Court) Using the Grace Period

To win your appeal based on this technicality, you need to provide evidence:
1. **Your Original Ticket**: A photo showing the expiry time.
2. **The PCN Photo**: Showing the "Time Seen From" and "Time Issued."
3. **The Logic**: Explicitly cite the "The Civil Enforcement of Parking Contraventions (England) General (Amendment) Regulations 2015" for council tickets.
