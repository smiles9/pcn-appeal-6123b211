## Fighting a Parking Fine in Canberra

**Parking enforcement in the ACT is managed by Access Canberra (Parking Operations) under the *Road Transport (General) Act 1999*.** Whether you were fined in Civic, Barton, or around the Australian National University (ANU), the process to contest it is managed centrally by the ACT Government.

This guide outlines how to navigate the ACT appeals system and the specific local factors that can help your case in Canberra.

## The Appeal Process: Access Canberra

In the ACT, the first step to contesting a parking fine is an **Internal Review**. This is a request for Access Canberra to reconsider the fine based on the evidence you provide.

### 1. Request an Internal Review

The most effective way to appeal is online via the **Access Canberra 'Traffic and Parking Infringements' portal**.

**What to include in your appeal:**

*   **The Infringement Number:** Found on your ticket.

*   **A clear explanation:** Why the fine should be withdrawn (e.g., "The ParkMobile app failed to process my payment").

*   **Supporting Evidence:** Photos of the scene, GPS logs, medical certificates, or towing receipts.

*   **A Statutory Declaration:** If you are nominating another driver or providing witness testimony.

### 2. Statutory Declarations

If you were not the driver, you must nominate the person who was. This is done through a formal Statutory Declaration. Failing to nominate the correct driver can lead to significant penalties for the registered owner.

### 3. ACT Magistrates Court Election

If your internal review is rejected, your final option is to **elect to go to court**. This moves the matter to the ACT Magistrates Court on Knowles Place. Only take this step if you have strong evidence that the fine was issued unlawfully, as the court can impose higher fines and legal costs.

## Common Grounds for Success in Canberra

The following situations often result in a withdrawal or a caution in the ACT:

1.  **Obscured Signage:** In areas like Braddon or the Parliamentary Triangle, signs can sometimes be obscured by temporary roadworks or trees. If you can prove the sign was not clearly visible, the fine is often withdrawn.

2.  **Vehicle Breakdown:** If your car suffered a mechanical failure (e.g., flat battery, engine failure). You must provide a receipt from a mechanic or a roadside assistance provider like the NRMA.

3.  **Medical Emergency:** If you had to park urgently to attend to a medical crisis. A letter from a doctor or hospital (e.g., Canberra Hospital) is required.

4.  **ParkMobile/Meter Failure:** If the parking meter was faulty or the ParkMobile app was malfunctioning. You must provide the meter number and evidence of the failure (e.g., a screenshot of the app error).

## Canberra Specifics: High-Enforcement Zones

*   **Civic (CBD):** High enforcement of 'No Stopping' and 'Loading Zones' during business hours (8:30 AM to 5:30 PM).

*   **Barton/Parkes (Parliamentary Triangle):** Parking is strictly monitored, especially during sitting weeks of Parliament.

*   **ANU Campus:** The university area is strictly monitored by both ANU security and Access Canberra. Ensure you have a valid permit or have paid through the correct app.

## What Happens Next?

Once you lodge your review, Access Canberra will place the fine **on hold**. You will receive a letter or email with one of three outcomes:

1.  **Penalty Withdrawn:** No fine to pay, no record of the offence.

2.  **Official Warning:** The offence is recorded, but you don't have to pay the fine. This is common for first-time offenders with valid excuses.

3.  **Penalty Confirmed:** You must pay the fine by the new due date or elect to go to court.
