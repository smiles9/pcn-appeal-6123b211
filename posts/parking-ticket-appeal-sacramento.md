# How to Contest a Parking Citation in Sacramento, CA

Parking in California's capital can be a challenge, and receiving a citation from the City of Sacramento Parking Services is a common occurrence. Fortunately, California state law provides a structured three-step process for contesting these tickets.

## Common Parking Citation Hotspots in Sacramento

Avoid getting ticketed by being extra careful in these high-enforcement areas:

*   **Midtown Sacramento:** Especially near popular restaurants and bars on J, K, and L Streets.
*   **Old Sacramento:** Heavy enforcement for time limits and loading zones.
*   **Golden 1 Center:** Temporary "No Parking" zones are strictly enforced during events.
*   **UC Davis Health Campus:** Watch for strict permit-only areas.

## The 3-Step Contest Process

In accordance with California Vehicle Code section 40215, you have three levels of appeal:

| Level | Description | Timeline |
|---
|---|---|
| **1. Initial Review** | An administrative review by Parking Services to check for errors. | Within 21 days of citation |
| **2. Administrative Hearing** | A hearing before an independent examiner (can be in-person, by mail, or online). | Within 21 days of Level 1 result |
| **3. Superior Court** | A de novo hearing in the Sacramento County Superior Court. | Within 30 days of Level 2 result |

## How to Submit Your Request

The easiest way to start your appeal is online.

*   **Online Portal:** [Contest a Sacramento Citation](https://www.cityofsacramento.gov/public-works/parking)
*   **Information Needed:** Citation number and license plate number.
*   **Payment Requirement:** You do *not* have to pay the fine to request an Initial Review. However, for an Administrative Hearing, you must pay the fine in full unless you qualify for a financial hardship waiver.

### |A| What are common grounds for a successful contest in Sacramento? |||
Successful appeals often cite missing or illegible signs, incorrect vehicle information on the ticket (wrong VIN or make), or proof of a valid permit/payment that the officer missed.

### |A| How long does the process take? |||
An Initial Review typically takes 4 to 6 weeks. If you proceed to an Administrative Hearing, it may take another month to schedule and receive a decision.

### |A| Can I get a payment plan if I can't afford the fine? |||
Yes, the City of Sacramento offers payment plans for low-income individuals. You must apply within 120 days of the citation being issued or 10 days after an administrative hearing decision.
