# How to Appeal a Parking Ticket in Sydney

If you’ve received a **Penalty Notice** in Sydney, you have the right to request a review through Revenue NSW. Most disputes are handled online or via the MyPenalty app.

## Sydney Parking Ticket Hotspots

Enforcement is very active in these high-demand zones:

*   **Sydney CBD / Circular Quay:** Extremely strict monitoring of time limits, loading zones, and clearways.

*   **Bondi Beach:** Heavy enforcement of short-term parking and residential permit areas, especially on weekends and holidays.

*   **Surry Hills:** High enforcement of "No Parking" and "No Stopping" zones during events and commute peaks.

*   **Newtown (King St):** Frequent ticketing for parking in bus zones or over stay limits.

## The Sydney Appeal Process

Revenue NSW manages parking fines for councils across the state.

1.  **Revenue NSW:** Go to the [Request a Review](https://www.revenue.nsw.gov.au/fines-and-fees/request-a-review) page to submit an online application.

2.  **MyPenalty App:** Use the app to view your fine, check the photo evidence taken by the ranger, and submit an appeal directly.

3.  **Submission:** You will need your penalty notice number and the date of the offence.

4.  **Evidence:** Attach photos of any broken meters, obscured signs, or your valid ticket/app payment.

## FAQ

|A| Can I ignore a Sydney parking ticket? ||| No. Revenue NSW will add late fees and can suspend your driver's license or car registration if the fine remains unpaid.

|A| How long does a review take? ||| Revenue NSW usually responds within 14 to 28 days.

|A| What if my review is denied? ||| You can elect to have the matter heard in the Local Court, though additional court costs may apply if you lose.
