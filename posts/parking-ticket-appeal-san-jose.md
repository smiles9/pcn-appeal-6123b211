# San Jose Parking Ticket Contest Guide

San Jose has some of the highest parking fines in California. However, the City of San Jose provides a clear three-step process for contesting a citation if you believe it was issued in error.

## The Three-Stage Appeal Process

In San Jose, you cannot jump straight to a judge. You must follow this sequence:

1.  **Administrative Review (Level 1)**: An internal review by the Department of Transportation. This is free and must be requested within 21 days.

2.  **Administrative Hearing (Level 2)**: If denied at Level 1, you can request a hearing with an independent officer. You must pay the fine amount as a deposit (waived for financial hardship).

3.  **Superior Court (Level 3)**: A final appeal in the Santa Clara County Superior Court (5 fee).

## San Jose Hotspots & Hazards

*   **SJSU Perimeter**: Residential permit zones around San Jose State University are strictly enforced.

*   **Downtown (S. 1st & 2nd St)**: High-frequency enforcement for expired meters and "No Parking" during peak hours.

*   **Santana Row**: While private, surrounding public streets are heavily monitored.

## Winning Strategies

*   **Broken Meters**: If a meter was broken, report it immediately to (408) 534-4141 and include the meter number in your appeal.

*   **Inaccurate Citation**: Check if the License Plate, Make, or Model on the ticket matches your vehicle. Any error here is grounds for dismissal.

*   **Signage Obstruction**: California law requires clear signage. If a tree or another sign blocked the "No Parking" notice, take a video showing the driver's perspective.
