## Appealing Secure Parking Notices in Australia

**Secure Parking is a major operator across Australia.** Their "Payment Notices" look official, but they are private invoices. 

This guide provides the legal framework to challenge these notices under **Australian Contract Law** and the **Australian Consumer Law (ACL)**.

## Key Defense Strategies

### 1. No Contract Formed
If the entrance signs are obscured or the terms are too complex to read while driving, you can argue that you never entered into a contract.

### 2. Penalty vs. Genuine Loss
If you overstayed in a car park where the rate is $10/hour, and they charge you $88, the additional $78 is a "penalty." Australian courts generally do not enforce penalty clauses in private contracts.

### 3. Misleading Conduct
If the car park signs suggest that the company has the power to "fine" you, this could be considered misleading or deceptive conduct under the ACL.

## Steps to Appeal

1. **Check the signs**: Take photos immediately.
2. **Draft your response**: Focus on "Liquidated Damages" and "Contract Law."
3. **Use the ACL**: Cite sections regarding unfair contract terms.
