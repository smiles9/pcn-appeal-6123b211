# Brampton Parking Ticket Appeal Guide

Brampton enforces strict parking bylaws to maintain traffic flow. If you've received a Penalty Notice, you can dispute it through the city's **Administrative Penalty System (APS)**.

## 1. Important Brampton Parking Bylaws

- **Winter Parking:** Between **November 15 and April 15**, parking on any city street is prohibited if it interferes with snow clearing, regardless of whether it is snowing.

- **Boulevard Parking:** You cannot park on the "boulevard" (the portion of the driveway between the sidewalk and the street).

- **3-Hour Limit:** Most residential streets have a default **3-hour parking limit** unless a permit or sign states otherwise.

## 2. Brampton Parking Ticket Hotspots

- **Sheridan College (Davis Campus):** Intense enforcement on surrounding residential streets like McLaughlin Rd.

- **Downtown Brampton (Garden Square):** High enforcement near the Rose Theatre and local municipal buildings.

- **Bramalea City Centre:** Private security frequently patrols the mall lots, but city tickets are common on perimeter roads.

- **Brampton Civic Hospital:** Constant monitoring of nearby residential 'No Parking' zones.

## 3. How to Dispute Your Brampton Ticket

You have **15 days** from the date the ticket was issued to request a **Screening Review**.

### The Dispute Workflow:

1. **Online Request:** Access the [Brampton Parking Dispute Portal]({{portal_url}}).

2. **Review Evidence:** The city often provides officer notes and photos online—review these before submitting your defense.

3. **Draft Your Statement:** Clearly explain why you believe the ticket was issued in error. Use facts, not emotions.

4. **The Screening Meeting:** Most screenings are now handled via phone or online form. A Screening Officer will determine if the ticket should be canceled or varied.
