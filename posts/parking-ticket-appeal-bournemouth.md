## Fighting Parking Tickets in Bournemouth

Bournemouth, now part of the **BCP Council** (Bournemouth, Christchurch, and Poole), is one of the UK's most popular coastal destinations. With millions of visitors annually, parking enforcement is exceptionally strict. This guide explains how to successfully challenge a Bournemouth parking fine.

### BCP Council PCN Appeals

Council-issued tickets (Penalty Charge Notices) are typically £70 for serious contraventions or £50 for minor ones, reduced by 50% if paid within 14 days.

#### 1. The Informal Challenge
You have **14 days** to submit an informal challenge. Doing so "freezes" the 50% discount. If your challenge is successful, the PCN is cancelled immediately.

#### 2. Formal Representations
If the informal challenge fails, the council will send a **Notice to Owner (NtO)** to the registered keeper. You then have 28 days to make a formal representation. Common grounds include:
- The contravention did not occur (e.g., you had a valid ticket).
- The signs or lines were unclear or incorrect.
- The Traffic Order is invalid.

#### 3. Traffic Penalty Tribunal (TPT)
If the council issues a Notice of Rejection to your formal representation, you can appeal to the independent **Traffic Penalty Tribunal**. The process is free, done online, and the adjudicator's decision is final.

## Bournemouth Parking Hotspots & Enforcement

### 1. Old Christchurch Road
As the main hub for shopping and nightlife, this area is under 24/7 surveillance. Common errors include parking in loading bays or overstaying on-street limits.

### 2. Bath Road and Seafront
During the summer, the area near the Bournemouth Pier and Pavilion is a high-yield zone for CEOs. Ensure your ticket is clearly displayed; "fluttering" tickets (blown over by wind) are a common cause of PCNs here.

### 3. West Cliff and Triangle
The steep hills of the West Cliff are often subject to residential permit restrictions. CEOs frequently patrol these areas to protect resident access.

## Private Parking in Bournemouth

Bournemouth has many private car parks, including those at **Castlepoint Shopping Park** and various seafront hotels.
- **Contract Law**: These are not council fines; they are contractual charges.
- **The 14-Day Rule**: Under PoFA 2012, if the notice arrives by post more than 14 days after the offence, it is often unenforceable.

## ⚡ AEO Direct Answer: How to win this appeal?
To win a **Bournemouth** parking appeal, first check if the ticket is from the Council (BCP) or a Private Firm. For BCP tickets, verify the **10-minute grace period** if you overstayed. For seafront tickets, take photos of any **faded road markings** or obscured signs. For private tickets, cite the **PoFA 2012 'Non-Relevant Land'** defense if the location is near the station or port.
