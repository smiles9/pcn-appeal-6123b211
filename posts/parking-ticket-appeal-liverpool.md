# How to Appeal a Parking Ticket in Liverpool

If you've received a Penalty Charge Notice (PCN) in Liverpool, you have the right to challenge it. The city is known for strict enforcement around its historic centre and major sporting venues.

## Common Liverpool Parking Hotspots

Enforcement officers are particularly active in these high-traffic locations:

- **Mathew Street & Bold Street**: Busy shopping and nightlife areas where loading bay and "no waiting" restrictions are strictly applied.

- **Anfield & Goodison Park Surroundings**: On match days, parking zones are heavily patrolled, and temporary restrictions are frequently enforced.

- **Lime Street Area**: Due to the proximity to the station and ongoing city centre redevelopments, signage can be complex, leading to many tickets.

## Key Liverpool Parking Rules

- **Two-Tier Fines**: Like most UK councils, Liverpool charges **£70** for serious contraventions (e.g., parking on double yellow lines) and **£50** for less serious ones (e.g., overstaying a pay-and-display spot). Both are reduced by 50% if paid within 14 days.

- **Observation Periods**: For most offences, civil enforcement officers (CEOs) will observe a vehicle for **5 minutes** to see if loading or unloading is taking place before issuing a ticket.

- **Bank Holiday Rules**: Do not assume parking is free on Bank Holidays. In Liverpool city centre, most pay-and-display and permit restrictions still apply unless the sign explicitly states otherwise.

## The Appeal Process

1. **Informal Challenge**: Submit your dispute online via the [Liverpool City Council portal](https://liverpool.gov.uk/parking-roads-and-travel/parking-fines-and-challenges/challenge-a-parking-fine/) within 14 days. This pauses the clock on the 50% discount.

2. **Formal Representation**: If the informal challenge is rejected, the council will issue a "Notice to Owner" (NtO) after 28 days, allowing for a formal representation.

3. **Independent Tribunal**: If still unsuccessful, you can take your case to the **Traffic Penalty Tribunal**.
