# How to Appeal a Parking Ticket in Auckland

If you’ve received an **Infringement Notice** in Auckland, you have the right to request a review. Most disputes are handled through Auckland Transport (AT) or private operators like Wilson Parking.

## Auckland Parking Ticket Hotspots

Enforcement is very active in these high-demand zones:

*   **Auckland CBD / Viaduct:** Extremely strict monitoring of time limits and permit-only areas.

*   **Newmarket:** Heavy enforcement of short-term parking around Broadway.

*   **Ponsonby:** Frequent ticketing for parking on nature strips or over driveways.

*   **Britomart:** High enforcement in loading zones and bus lanes.

## The Auckland Appeal Process

Auckland Transport manages most public parking. Private lots (like Wilson or Care Park) issue "Breach Notices" which have different rules.

1.  **Auckland Transport (AT):** Go to the [AT Parking Fines Page](https://at.govt.nz/driving-parking/parking-fines-infringements/) to submit an online explanation.

2.  **Private Lots:** If you receive a notice from a private operator, you can appeal through their specific portal (e.g., [PES NZ](https://pesnz.co.nz/)).

3.  **Submission:** You will need your infringement number and vehicle registration.

4.  **Evidence:** Attach photos of any broken meters, obscured signs, or your valid ticket/app payment.

## Key Defenses

| Defense | Strategy |

| :--- | :--- |

| **Broken Meter** | Take a photo of the meter's serial number and the error message on the screen. |

| **Medical Emergency** | Provide a dated certificate or letter from the hospital/doctor. |

| **Vehicle Breakdown** | Provide a copy of the towing receipt or AA breakdown report. |

## FAQ

|A| Can I ignore an Auckland parking ticket? ||| No. AT tickets are transferred to the Ministry of Justice (Court) after 28 days, which adds significant costs.

|A| How long does an online appeal take? ||| AT usually responds within 10 to 15 working days.

|A| What if my appeal is denied? ||| You can request a "Court Hearing" to present your case before a judge, though costs may increase if you lose.

To win an appeal against **Auckland** parking fines, the most effective strategy is citing **Statutory Non-Compliance**. If the ticket was issued on airport land, use the **'Non-Relevant Land'** defense under POFA 2012. If it's a private lot, check for **signage visibility** and the mandatory **10-minute grace period**.
