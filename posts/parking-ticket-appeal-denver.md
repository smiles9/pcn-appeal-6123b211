## Fighting a Parking Ticket in Denver

**Parking enforcement in Denver is managed by the Department of Transportation & Infrastructure (DOTI), with all formal court disputes handled by the Denver County Court.** Denver is known for its aggressive enforcement, especially regarding street sweeping, meter expiration, and resident permit zones.

This guide explains your legal rights and the specific steps needed to successfully appeal a parking ticket in the Mile High City.

## Your Response Options: Online Dispute vs. Court Hearing

When you receive a Denver parking ticket, you have two main ways to contest it:

### 1. Online Dispute (Informal Review)

This is the recommended first step for most drivers. You can submit an explanation and evidence through the **DenverGov.org 'Parking In Denver' portal**.

*   **Best for:** Clear errors, faulty meters, or fixed equipment issues.

*   **Process:** An administrative reviewer will look at your case and the officer's notes.

*   **Result:** The ticket is either dismissed, reduced, or the dispute is denied.

### 2. Final Hearing (Denver County Court)

If your online dispute is denied or if you prefer a formal legal proceeding, you can request a **Final Hearing**.

*   **Process:** You must appear in person at **1437 Bannock St., Room 140**, Denver, CO 80202, between 8:00 AM and 4:00 PM, Monday through Friday, to schedule your hearing.

*   **Best for:** Contesting the legality of a sign, arguing constitutional issues, or when you have witness testimony.

*   **Result:** A judicial officer's decision is final. If you lose, you may have to pay court costs in addition to the fine.

## How to Appeal: Step-by-Step

### Step 1: Dispute Within 20 Days

You must take action within 20 days of the ticket date. If you wait longer, late fees will be added and your right to a hearing may be waived.

### Step 2: Gather Evidence

Denver's ROWE agents (Right of Way Enforcement) take photos of every violation. You should take your own photos to counter theirs:

*   **Photos:** Show the lack of signage, faded curb paint, or a malfunctioning meter screen.

*   **Time Stamps:** Use your phone's GPS or a timestamp app to prove when you were parked.

*   **Repair Receipts:** If you fixed a registration or equipment issue that led to the ticket.

### Step 3: Present Your Case

Whether online or in court, focus on the **facts** of the ordinance. Denver's Revised Municipal Code (DRMC) is what the judicial officer will use. Avoid emotional arguments (e.g., "I'm having a bad day") and stick to legal ones (e.g., "The sign was not placed in accordance with DRMC Section 54-486").

## Common Grounds for Success in Denver

The following defenses are frequently successful in Denver:

1.  **Faulty Meter/Pay Station:** If the meter was broken or the 'ParkMobile' app was down. Denver ROWE agents usually check meter status, but if you have a video of a 'Fail' message, you have a strong case.

2.  **Missing or Obscured Signage:** Denver's many '1-hour' and '2-hour' zones can be confusing. If a sign was knocked down or obscured by a delivery truck, the ticket should be dismissed.

3.  **Medical Emergency:** If you were forced to park illegally due to an urgent medical crisis. Provide documentation from Denver Health or a local hospital.

4.  **Incorrect Vehicle Info:** If the agent recorded the wrong license plate or car make, the ticket is often dismissed for 'error on face of citation'.

## Denver Specifics: Street Sweeping and The Boot

*   **Street Sweeping:** Denver's street sweeping season runs from **April 1st to November 30th**. These are $50 fines and are the most common source of tickets. Always check the red-and-white signs on your block.

*   **The Boot:** If you have **three or more unpaid parking tickets**, your vehicle is eligible for a boot (a wheel lock). To get the boot removed, you must pay all fines plus a $100 'boot fee' immediately.

## What Happens Next?

If you win your hearing, the ticket is dismissed and you owe nothing. If you lose, you must pay the fine by the date set by the court. If you fail to pay, the matter will be sent to a collection agency, and your credit score may be affected.
