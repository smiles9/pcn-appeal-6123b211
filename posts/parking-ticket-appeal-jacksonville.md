## Parking Tickets in Jacksonville: What You Need to Know

**Jacksonville is the largest city by area in the contiguous United States, and its parking enforcement is just as expansive. From Downtown to San Marco, Riverside, and the beaches, the City of Jacksonville’s Office of Public Parking and the Jacksonville Sheriff’s Office (JSO) are highly active.**

Parking violations in Jacksonville are civil matters adjudicated through the **Office of Public Parking**. The city uses an administrative review and hearing process, which is a formal review of the facts and evidence by a trained hearing officer.

> **Key stat**: Jacksonville has a very short "15-day window" for appeals. If you wait more than two weeks, your fine will increase, and you will lose your right to a hearing.

## How Jacksonville Parking Enforcement Works

**Enforcement is handled by the Jacksonville Office of Public Parking and the Jacksonville Sheriff’s Office. Meters are generally enforced Monday through Friday, from 8:00 AM to 6:00 PM, though some areas vary.**

Common ticket types and fine amounts in Jacksonville:

1. **Expired Meter** — $25

2. **Loading Zone Violation** — $35

3. **No Parking/Standing Zone** — $35

4. **Fire Hydrant** — $50

5. **Bus Zone Violation** — $50

6. **Handicap Space Violation** — $250

7. **Blocking Driveway/Sidewalk** — $35

## The Review and Hearing Process

**If you wish to dispute a ticket, you must request a review. Jacksonville offers several ways to handle this process:**

1. **The Request**: You must request the review within 15 days.

2. **The Review**: A hearing officer reviews the citation and your evidence. This can often be done without an in-person appearance.

3. **The Evidence**: Jacksonville hearing officers place a high value on photographic evidence and time-stamped receipts from parking apps like **MyJAX**.

4. **The Appeal**: If you lose at the administrative level, you can appeal to a County Court Judge within 30 days.

## How to Appeal a Jacksonville Parking Ticket

### Step 1: Document Your Case Immediately

**Downtown Jacksonville has very specific parking rules that can change by the block. If you get a ticket, take photos of where you parked, the nearest signs, and the parking meter.** If you used a parking app, save a screenshot of your active session.

### Step 2: Submit Your Appeal

You must submit your appeal within **15 calendar days** of the ticket date.

- **Online**: Visit jacksonville.gov and use the "Pay or Dispute" portal to upload your appeal.

- **By Phone**: You can call 904-255-5858 to inquire about your options.

- **In Person**: Visit the Office of Public Parking at 214 North Hogan St. (8:00 AM - 5:00 PM).

### Step 3: Present Your Defense

During your hearing, be concise and stick to the facts. If you were ticketed for an expired meter but the meter was broken, providing a photo of the "Out of Order" message or a service report number is your best defense.

## Strongest Grounds for Appeal in Jacksonville

**Jacksonville hearing officers are generally reasonable if you can prove a specific error or a technical failure.**

1. **Broken Meter**: If a meter or kiosk failed to accept payment, provide a photo and a report number from the City’s meter maintenance line.

2. **Inadequate Signage**: If a "No Parking" sign was missing, damaged, or not visible from where you parked, provide photos.

3. **Factual Error**: If the ticket lists the wrong plate, make/model, or location, the citation is technically invalid.

4. **Valid Permit**: If you have a valid residential or disability permit and it was properly displayed, provide proof.

5. **Emergency Situations**: Documented medical emergencies or vehicle breakdowns (with a tow receipt) are often considered valid defenses.

## Jacksonville-Specific Legal Points

**Parking is governed by the Jacksonville City Ordinance (Chapter 804). Key points to remember:**

1. **The 15-Day Rule**: This is the most important rule in Jacksonville. Do not miss this deadline, or your fine will increase and you will lose your right to a hearing.

2. **Beach Area Rules**: Be extra careful in Jacksonville Beach on weekends. They have specialized enforcement teams focused on beach access and safety.

3. **Event Parking**: During major events at the stadium or arena, parking rates and rules can change. Always check the sign or the app.
