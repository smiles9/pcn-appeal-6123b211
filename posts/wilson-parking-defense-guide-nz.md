## Appealing Wilson Parking Notices in NZ

**Wilson Parking is the largest operator in New Zealand.** Their notices are often called "Breach Notices" or "Payment Notices."

## The Legal Landscape in New Zealand

Private parking enforcement in NZ is governed by the **Fair Trading Act** and **Contract Law**.

1. **Not a Fine**: Private companies have no statutory power to fine you.
2. **Breach of Contract**: They claim you broke the contract by not paying or overstaying.
3. **The Penalty Rule**: If the amount they charge is a "penalty" (meant to punish rather than compensate), it is unenforceable under NZ law.

## Winning Grounds in NZ

### 1. Inadequate Signage
If the signs are poorly lit, hidden, or have tiny print, you can argue that you never agreed to the contract.

### 2. Excessive Charges
If the parking fee was $5/hour and they charge you $65, the additional $60 is likely a penalty. NZ courts have previously ruled against excessive "liquidated damages" in parking cases.

### 3. Fair Trading Act
If the signs are misleading (e.g., they look like official council signs), the company may be in breach of the Fair Trading Act.

## The NZ Appeal Process

1. **Direct Appeal**: Use the operator's website (e.g., PES NZ for Wilson). 
2. **Disputes Tribunal**: If the operator persists, you can take the matter to the Disputes Tribunal. This costs a small fee but is a formal way to have a referee decide the case.
