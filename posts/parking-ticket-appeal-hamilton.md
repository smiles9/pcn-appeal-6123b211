# How to Appeal a Parking Ticket in Hamilton

If you've received a "Penalty Notice" in Hamilton, Ontario, you are part of the city's **Administrative Penalty System (APS)**. This system allows you to challenge your ticket through a formal screening and hearing process without the need to go to Provincial Court.

## Hamilton Parking Ticket Hotspots
According to recent City of Hamilton data, these are the most ticketed areas:
*   **King Street West:** The #1 hotspot in the city with over 5,000 tickets issued annually.
*   **TD Coliseum (FirstOntario Centre):** Massive enforcement during concerts and Bulldogs games on York Blvd and Bay Street.
*   **Downtown Core (John St N, James St N):** Active ticketing for meter expiration and loading zone violations.
*   **Charlton Ave E:** High enforcement near St. Joseph's Healthcare Hamilton.

## The Hamilton Appeal Process
You must act within **15 days** from the date the Penalty Notice was issued.

1.  **Request a Screening:** You can request a screening review online or via email to [parking.screening@hamilton.ca](mailto:parking.screening@hamilton.ca).
2.  **Screening Review:** A Screening Officer will review your submission. They have the power to cancel, reduce, or uphold the Penalty Notice.
3.  **Request a Hearing:** If you disagree with the Screening Officer's decision, you can request a further review by a Hearing Officer (which is final).

## Successful Appeal Strategies
| Strategy | Documentation Needed |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Observation Rules** | Many violations require a 5 or 10-minute observation period. Check if the ticket shows this. |
| **Inaccurate Ticket** | If the ticket has the wrong license plate, make, or color, it may be void. |
| **Mechanical Breakdown** | Provide a tow truck receipt or a repair bill dated the day of the ticket. |

## FAQ
|A| Can I ignore a Hamilton Penalty Notice? ||| No. Failure to pay or dispute will lead to late fees and eventually prevent your license plate renewal with the MTO.
|A| What is the "Toys for Tickets" program? ||| In December, the City often allows you to pay your parking fine with a new, unwrapped toy of equal or greater value.
|A| How much is a typical Hamilton parking fine? ||| Most common infractions range from $35 to $60, though unauthorized parking in accessible spots is significantly higher.
