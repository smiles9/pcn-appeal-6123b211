## Parking Tickets in Houston: What You Need to Know

**Houston issues hundreds of thousands of parking tickets annually through ParkHouston and the Houston Police Department. While fines are generally lower than in coastal cities, Houston's sprawling urban landscape and growing metered zones mean more drivers are facing citations every year.** The city has significantly expanded its metered parking areas in recent years.

Parking enforcement in Houston is managed primarily by ParkHouston, the city's parking management division, along with Houston Police Department officers for certain violations. Citations are issued under the Houston Code of Ordinances (Chapter 26) and the Texas Transportation Code.

> **Key stat**: Houston's metered parking program has expanded significantly, with ParkHouston managing over 7,000 meters across Downtown, Midtown, and surrounding neighborhoods.

## How Houston Parking Enforcement Works

**ParkHouston parking enforcement officers patrol metered zones in Downtown, Midtown, Montrose, EaDo, and the Museum District, while HPD handles violations in other areas.** Citations are issued under the Houston Code of Ordinances, Chapter 26.

Common ticket types include:

1. **Expired meter** — $30-$40
2. **No parking zone** — $40-$50
3. **Fire lane** — $75
4. **Disabled parking** — up to $500 (Texas Transportation Code 681)
5. **Overtime parking** — $30
6. **Blocking driveway** — $50

## Private Parking Hotspots in Houston

**Private parking lots and garages are widespread in Houston, particularly in the Galleria area, Medical Center, Downtown, and near sports venues like Minute Maid Park and NRG Stadium.** Companies like LAZ Parking, SP+, and Ace Parking operate hundreds of facilities.

Private parking charges are contractual and cannot result in city fines or DMV holds. Disputes should be addressed directly with the operator or through the Texas Attorney General's consumer protection division.

## How to Appeal a Houston Parking Ticket

### Step 1: Administrative Review

Contact ParkHouston within **10 business days** of the citation for an administrative review:
- **Online** through the ParkHouston portal
- **By phone** — call ParkHouston customer service
- **In person** — visit the ParkHouston office

### Step 2: Municipal Court Hearing

If the administrative review is denied or you prefer a formal hearing, request one through **Houston Municipal Court**. You can:
- Appear before a judge or hearing officer
- Present photographs, receipts, and witness testimony
- Receive a decision at the hearing

### Step 3: Appeal

If the municipal court finds against you, you can appeal to a higher court within the timeframe specified in the judgment.

> **Success tip**: ParkHouston meters accept the ParkMobile app. If you received a ticket while having an active ParkMobile session, your app payment history is strong evidence for dismissal.

## Strongest Grounds for Appeal in Houston

**The most effective Houston parking ticket defenses rely on meter malfunctions, missing signage, payment app records, and factual errors on the citation.** Top grounds include:

1. **Meter malfunction** — broken meter or payment kiosk that failed to accept payment
2. **Missing or unclear signs** — parking restrictions must be clearly posted
3. **Active mobile payment** — valid ParkMobile session covering the time of citation
4. **Factual errors** — wrong license plate, vehicle description, or location
5. **Medical emergency** — documented emergency prevented moving the vehicle
6. **Disabled parking permit** — valid placard or plate was properly displayed

## Houston-Specific Legal Points

**The Houston Code of Ordinances, Chapter 26, governs city parking regulations, with the Texas Transportation Code providing statewide authority for disabled parking (Ch. 681) and towing (Ch. 682).** Key legal details:

1. **ParkMobile integration**: Houston meters work with the ParkMobile app. App transaction records are valid evidence in disputes. Ensure your session was active for the correct zone and time.

2. **No booting program**: Unlike Chicago and NYC, Houston does not have an aggressive vehicle booting program for unpaid parking tickets. The primary enforcement mechanism is DMV registration holds.

3. **Disabled parking**: Texas Transportation Code Chapter 681 provides statewide disabled parking enforcement with fines up to $500. Fraudulent use of disabled placards carries additional criminal penalties.
