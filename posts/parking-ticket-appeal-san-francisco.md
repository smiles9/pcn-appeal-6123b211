# San Francisco (SFMTA) Parking Ticket Appeal Guide (2026)

San Francisco is notorious for its aggressive parking enforcement, managed by the **SFMTA (San Francisco Municipal Transportation Agency)**. With steep fines for street cleaning and meter expirations, thousands of citations are issued daily. However, many of these can be successfully "protested" (appealed) if you know the local rules.

## San Francisco Parking Hotspots (2026 Alert)

SFMTA "Meter Maids" are exceptionally active in these high-traffic zones:

- **Fisherman's Wharf / Embarcardero**: Constant patrolling for expired meters and restricted commercial loading zones.

- **Union Square / Financial District**: Strict enforcement of tow-away zones during peak commute hours.

- **The Mission District**: High frequency of citations for "perpendicular parking" where not allowed and residential permit violations.

- **Lombard Street / Russian Hill**: Frequent ticketing for "wheels not curbed" on hills with a grade of 3% or more.

## Top Grounds for Dismissal in San Francisco

### 1. The 5-Minute Meter Grace Period

San Francisco policy generally allows for a **5-minute grace period** for muni-meters. If your citation was issued within 5 minutes of the expiration time shown on your receipt, you should protest immediately.

### 2. "Curbed Wheels" Inaccuracy

Under **CVC 22509**, you must turn your wheels to the curb when parking on a hill. However, SFMTA officers sometimes issue these incorrectly on flatter streets. If your vehicle was on a street with less than a 3% grade, the ticket is invalid.

### 3. Signage Deficiencies (CVC 22500)

If a street cleaning sign was missing, obscured by a tree, or the instructions were contradictory, the citation violates the **California Vehicle Code**. Photo evidence of the obscured sign is essential for a win.

### 4. Broken Meter

If the meter was non-functional and you attempted to pay, you are often exempt from the citation. Make sure to note the meter ID number and take a video of the malfunction.

## How to Protest Your SF Citation

### Online Protest (Step 1: Administrative Review)

Submit your protest within 21 days at the [SFMTA Citation Portal](https://www.sfmta.com/getting-around/drive-park/citations). Upload clear photos of your defense (meter receipts, obscured signs, etc.).

### Administrative Hearing (Step 2)

If your initial protest is rejected, you can request an Administrative Hearing. This is often where most tickets get dismissed as a hearing officer reviews the evidence more closely than the automated first-step system.
