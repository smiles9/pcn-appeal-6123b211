## Parking Tickets in Lyon: What You Need to Know

**Lyon — France's second-largest metropolitan area and a UNESCO World Heritage city — has active parking enforcement across the Presqu'île, Vieux Lyon, and the modern Part-Dieu business district.** Lyon Métropole issues the FPS (Forfait de Post-Stationnement) at €35, lower than Paris but still a significant cost for unpaid or expired parking.

Lyon follows the national Code de la route and the 2018 FPS system, with enforcement managed through Lyon Métropole and contracted operators who patrol the city's nine arrondissements.

> **Key stat**: Lyon's Presqu'île — the narrow peninsula between the Rhône and Saône rivers — concentrates the city's highest parking demand into one of its smallest geographic areas, driving intense enforcement.

**Lyon Métropole contracts with private enforcement operators who deploy agents with handheld devices and LAPI camera vehicles to verify parking payments across the city.**

## High-Enforcement Areas in Lyon

### Presqu'île

**The Presqu'île between the Rhône and Saône is Lyon's most heavily enforced parking area.** The streets around Place Bellecour (one of Europe's largest public squares), Rue de la République, and Place des Terreaux have the highest meter rates and shortest maximum stays. The Rue Victor Hugo and Rue Édouard Herriot shopping streets generate heavy parking demand. Enforcement agents patrol continuously during metered hours (Monday-Saturday, 9:00-19:00). Underground car parks (Parking Bellecour, Parking Cordeliers, Parking République) are strongly recommended.

### Vieux Lyon

The historic Vieux Lyon district on the west bank of the Saône is a UNESCO World Heritage Site with extremely limited vehicle access. The narrow Renaissance-era streets around the Cathédrale Saint-Jean and Rue du Boeuf are largely pedestrianised. Parking on the quays along the Saône (Quai Romain Rolland, Quai Fulchiron) is time-limited and actively enforced. The Parking Saint-Jean underground car park serves visitors.

### Part-Dieu

Lyon's main business and railway district (Part-Dieu) in the 3rd arrondissement has extensive metered parking with 2-hour maximum stays during business hours. The area around the Gare de Lyon Part-Dieu and the Part-Dieu shopping centre attracts commuter traffic, and enforcement is consistent. Surrounding residential streets have resident-priority parking (stationnement résidentiel).

### Confluence

The modern Confluence district at the southern tip of the Presqu'île (where the Rhône and Saône meet) has structured parking management with underground car parks beneath the shopping centre and Musée des Confluences. Street parking in the new development areas is metered and actively enforced.

## How to Appeal a Lyon Parking Ticket (FPS)

### Step 1: Identify the Ticket Type

Confirm whether your ticket is an FPS (Forfait de Post-Stationnement, €35) or a traffic fine (amende/contravention). The FPS shows "Forfait de Post-Stationnement" and follows the RAPO appeal process. Traffic fines show "Avis de Contravention" and are appealed through the OMP (Officier du Ministère Public).

### Step 2: File a RAPO

Submit a **RAPO (Recours Administratif Préalable Obligatoire)** within **one month** of the FPS date:

- **Online** at antai.gouv.fr

- **By post** to the address on the FPS

Include the FPS reference number, your carte grise (vehicle registration), and clear grounds with evidence.

### Step 3: CCSP Appeal

If the RAPO is rejected, appeal to the **CCSP (Commission du Contentieux du Stationnement Payant)** in Limoges within **one month** of rejection. You must **pay the FPS first**. If successful, the payment is refunded.

## Strong Grounds for Appeal in Lyon

1. **Wrong vehicle identification** — Verify the license plate on the FPS. LAPI camera errors occur, particularly with foreign plates and motorcycles.

2. **Valid app payment** — If you paid via PayByPhone but the system had a delay, provide payment confirmation with timestamp.

3. **Meter malfunction** — If the horodateur was out of service, document with photos. Note whether card and coin slots were both non-functional.

4. **Vehicle sold or stolen** — Provide the certificat de cession (sale certificate) or police report (dépôt de plainte).

5. **Fête des Lumières temporary zones** — If temporary restrictions were posted with insufficient notice, this supports an appeal. Document sign placement dates.

## Lyon-Specific Legal Points

1. **ZFE (Zone à Faibles Émissions)**: Lyon's low emission zone requires a Crit'Air vignette. Non-compliant vehicles face a €68 fine. This is separate from parking enforcement but affects legality of parking within the zone.

2. **TCL tram/bus lanes**: Lyon's extensive TCL tram and bus network includes dedicated lanes on major arteries like Cours de la Liberté and Avenue Berthelot. Stopping or parking in these lanes results in a €135 fine, enforced by camera.

3. **Quayside parking**: The Saône and Rhône quays are popular parking areas but have variable restrictions. Some quays are converted to pedestrian/cycling paths during summer (Voies sur Berges), eliminating parking entirely. Check current seasonal status before parking.
