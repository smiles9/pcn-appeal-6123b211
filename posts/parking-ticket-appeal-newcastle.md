## Fighting a Parking Fine in Newcastle

**Parking enforcement in Newcastle is managed by the City of Newcastle, but the processing and appeals are handled centrally by Revenue NSW.** This means that whether you were fined in the CBD, at Bar Beach, or near the University of Newcastle, the process to contest it is the same.

This guide outlines how to navigate the NSW appeals system and the specific local factors that can help your case in Newcastle.

## The Appeal Process: Revenue NSW

Most parking fines in Newcastle are 'Penalty Notices' issued under state legislation. To appeal, you must go through Revenue NSW.

### 1. Request a Review

The most effective way to appeal is online via the **Revenue NSW 'Service NSW' portal**.

**What to include in your appeal:**

*   **The Penalty Notice Number:** Found at the top of your ticket.

*   **A clear explanation:** Why the fine should be withdrawn (e.g., "The sign was obscured by a tree").

*   **Supporting Evidence:** Photos of the scene, GPS logs, or a statutory declaration from a witness.

*   **Your Driving Record:** If you have had a clean record for the last 10 years, you may be eligible for a caution under the NSW 'Ten-Year Rule'.

### 2. Nomination Notice

If you were not the driver, you must nominate the person who was. This is done through the same Revenue NSW portal. Failing to nominate the correct driver can lead to significant penalties for the registered owner.

### 3. Court Election

If your initial review is rejected, your final option is to **elect to go to court**. This moves the matter to the NSW Local Court (Newcastle Local Court on Church Street). Only take this step if you have strong evidence that the fine was issued unlawfully, as the court can impose higher fines and professional costs.

## Common Grounds for Success in Newcastle

Revenue NSW follows strict guidelines for withdrawing fines. The following are the most common successful grounds:

1.  **Obscured or Missing Signage:** In areas like Hamilton (Beaumont St) or Darby Street, signage can sometimes be hidden by shop awnings or delivery trucks. If you can prove the sign was not visible, the fine is often withdrawn.

2.  **Vehicle Breakdown:** If your car suffered a flat battery, tire, or engine failure in a restricted zone. You must provide a receipt from a mechanic or a roadside assistance provider like the NRMA.

3.  **Medical Emergency:** If you had to park urgently to attend to a medical crisis. A letter from a doctor or hospital is required.

4.  **The 'Ten-Year Rule':** In NSW, if you have not had a traffic or parking offence in the last 10 years, you can request a 'caution' instead of a fine for minor offences (e.g., overstaying a meter by less than 15 minutes).

## Newcastle Specifics: Where People Get Fined

*   **Newcastle CBD (Hunter St):** High enforcement of 'No Stopping' and 'Loading Zones' during business hours.

*   **Beachfronts (Nobby's, Bar Beach):** Rangers are very active here on weekends and public holidays. Check for 'Special Event' parking signs.

*   **University of Newcastle (Callaghan):** The University has its own parking rules and enforcement, but appeals are still generally directed to Revenue NSW.

## What Happens Next?

Once you lodge your review, Revenue NSW will place the fine **on hold**. You do not need to pay anything while they investigate. You will receive a letter or email with one of three outcomes:

1.  **Penalty Withdrawn:** No fine to pay, no demerit points (if applicable).

2.  **Caution Issued:** The offence is recorded, but you don't have to pay the fine. This is common for first-time offenders or those with a clean 10-year record.

3.  **Penalty Confirmed:** You must pay the fine by the new due date or elect to go to court.
