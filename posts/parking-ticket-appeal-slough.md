# Slough Parking Ticket Appeal Guide (2026)

If you've received a Penalty Charge Notice (PCN) in Slough, you're not alone. With major regeneration projects and high-density parking zones, Slough remains a hotspot for parking enforcement. This guide covers how to dispute Slough Borough Council tickets and private parking charges.

## Slough Parking Hotspots
Be extra cautious in these areas:
- **Slough High Street & The Curve**: High enforcement for loading bay and bus lane violations.
- **Queensmere Observatory Shopping Centre**: Frequent private parking disputes.
- **Slough Station (Railway Terrace)**: Dropping off without a valid permit or outside designated areas.
- **Bath Road Retail Parks**: Strict enforcement of time limits.

## How to Appeal a Slough Council PCN
1. **Don't Pay Immediately**: Paying is considered an admission of guilt and closes the case.
2. **Informal Challenge**: Submit within 14 days to preserve the 50% discount if the appeal is rejected.
3. **Formal Representation**: If the informal challenge is rejected, wait for the Notice to Owner (NtO).

**Council Portal**: [Slough PCN Appeal Portal](https://www.slough.gov.uk/parking)

## Common Appeal Grounds in Slough
- **Faded Road Markings**: Common in residential permit zones near the town centre.
- **Signage Obstruction**: Signs obscured by foliage or street furniture near the Trading Estate.
- **Grace Period**: UK law allows a 10-minute grace period after your paid time expires.
