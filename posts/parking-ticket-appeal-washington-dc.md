## Parking Tickets in Washington DC: What You Need to Know

**Washington DC issues over 1.5 million parking tickets annually, generating approximately $100 million in revenue. The District's complex web of rush hour restrictions, residential permit zones, and federal jurisdiction areas makes it one of the most challenging cities for parking in the United States.** Visitors and residents alike face steep fines for violations.

DC parking enforcement is handled by the DC Department of Public Works (DPW) and the Metropolitan Police Department (MPD). Adjudication is managed through the DC DMV. Citations are issued under DC Municipal Regulations (DCMR) Title 18.

> **Key stat**: DC has one of the highest per-capita rates of parking ticket issuance in the country, with more tickets than registered vehicles each year.

## How DC Parking Enforcement Works

**DC parking enforcement officers patrol aggressively in Georgetown, Dupont Circle, Adams Morgan, Capitol Hill, and the National Mall area, with rush hour tow zones creating high-dollar violations on major corridors.** Citations are issued under DCMR Title 18.

Common ticket types include:

1. **Expired meter** — $50

2. **Residential permit zone** — $50

3. **No parking zone** — $100

4. **Rush hour violation** — $150 (plus towing)

5. **Street sweeping** — $45

6. **Fire hydrant** — $100

7. **Blocking crosswalk** — $100

## Private Parking Hotspots in DC

**Private parking garages and lots are prevalent throughout Downtown DC, Georgetown, and near the National Mall, often charging premium rates that rival Manhattan.** Operators like Colonial Parking, SP+, and PMI manage hundreds of facilities in the District.

Private parking charges are contractual disputes and are not adjudicated through the DC DMV. Disputes should be directed to the operator or pursued through DC consumer protection channels.

## How to Appeal a DC Parking Ticket

### Step 1: Contest Within 30 Days

File a contest within **30 calendar days** of the ticket date:

- **Online** at dmv.dc.gov — submit evidence and written defense

- **By mail** — send the contest form with documentation

- **In person** — request a hearing at a DC DMV adjudication center

### Step 2: Adjudication Hearing

A DC DMV hearing examiner reviews your case. You can present:

- Photographs of signs, meters, and the location

- Receipts or parking app records

- Written arguments citing DCMR Title 18

### Step 3: Reconsideration or Superior Court

If the decision is unfavorable, you can request **reconsideration** within 30 days, or file an appeal with DC Superior Court.

> **Success tip**: Rush hour tow zone tickets are among the most expensive in DC. Photograph the signs carefully — many rush hour restriction signs are obscured by trees or construction, providing strong appeal grounds.

## Strongest Grounds for Appeal in DC

**The most successful DC parking ticket appeals focus on signage problems, meter malfunctions, rush hour sign confusion, and procedural errors.** Top grounds include:

1. **Missing or obscured signs** — construction frequently blocks or removes parking signs in DC

2. **Meter malfunction** — broken meter or failed ParkMobile payment

3. **Rush hour sign confusion** — complex variable-time signs that are unclear or contradictory

4. **Factual errors on ticket** — wrong plate number, location, or violation code

5. **Federal vs. District jurisdiction** — some federal land (NPS) has different parking rules

6. **Valid permit displayed** — RPP permit or visitor pass was properly displayed

## DC-Specific Legal Points

**DC Municipal Regulations Title 18 governs all parking enforcement in the District, with additional authority under DC Code Title 50 (Motor Vehicles and Traffic).** Key legal details:

1. **Rock Creek Park**: Rock Creek Parkway and Beach Drive have directional rush hour restrictions enforced by the National Park Service and DC. Signs can be confusing; photograph any ambiguous signage.

2. **Booting program**: DC boots vehicles with two or more unpaid tickets over 60 days past due. The boot fee is $100. Vehicles with $1,000 or more in outstanding tickets may be towed and impounded.

3. **Federal jurisdiction**: Parking on National Park Service land (including the National Mall) is enforced by US Park Police under federal regulations, not DCMR. Federal citations are adjudicated in federal court, not DC DMV.
