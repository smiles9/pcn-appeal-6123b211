# POPLA Appeal Success Rates: Can You Actually Win?

One of the most common questions we get is: *"Is it even worth appealing to POPLA, or will they just side with the parking company?"* 

According to the latest data for early 2026, the success rate for motorists at **POPLA (Parking on Private Land Appeals)** remains high for those who use **technical legal defenses** rather than "mitigating circumstances."

## 1. The 2026 Success Benchmarks
*   **Overall Success Rate**: Approx. **42% - 48%** of appeals are upheld (motorist wins).
*   **Technical Defense Success Rate**: Over **85%** of appeals citing "No Keeper Liability" (Airport Byelaws) or "Non-compliant Notice to Keeper (NTK)" are successful.
*   **Mitigation Success Rate**: Less than **5%**. POPLA does not have the power to cancel a ticket because you "didn't see the sign" or "had a family emergency."

## 2. Why Motorists Win at POPLA
The Lead Adjudicator at POPLA recently highlighted the most common reasons for successful appeals:
1.  **POFA 2012 Non-Compliance**: The parking company failed to issue the Notice to Keeper within the 14-day window.
2.  **Inadequate Signage**: The signs were too high, too small, or unlit, meaning no contract was formed.
3.  **No Landowner Authority**: The parking company could not produce a contract proving they have the right to issue fines on behalf of the landowner.

## 3. The "Byelaw Advantage" for 2026
In 2026, the "Byelaw Defense" (Non-relevant land) has become the most effective tool for airport PCNs (APCOA at Heathrow/Manchester, NCP at Gatwick). Since POPLA adjudicators must follow the law, they are increasingly forced to uphold appeals where the parking company has incorrectly invoked POFA 2012 on airport land.

## 4. How to Maximize Your Odds
- **Never admit to being the driver.**
- **Upload photos of the signs (or lack thereof).**
- **Use technical language**: Focus on "Statutory Compliance" rather than "Fairness."
