# Adelaide Parking Ticket Appeal Guide

If you've received a parking fine (Expiation Notice) in the City of Adelaide or North Adelaide, you have the right to request a formal review. In 2026, the process is streamlined through the City of Adelaide's online portal.

## The Review Process: 'Review of Expiation'

In South Australia, parking fines are technically "Expiation Notices." To challenge one, you must submit a **Request for Review** before the due date.

## Adelaide Parking Hotspots

Be extra cautious in these high-enforcement areas:

*   **North Adelaide (O'Connell & Tynte St)**: High turnover areas with strict time limits.

*   **CBD Residential Zones**: Heavily monitored for permit compliance.

*   **Rundle Street**: Frequently patrolled during evening and weekend peak hours.

## Valid Grounds for Appeal in Adelaide

*   **Medical Emergency**: Provide a medical certificate or hospital admission record.

*   **Vehicle Breakdown**: Provide a receipt from a towing company or roadside assistance (RAA).

*   **Signage Issues**: If signs were obscured by trees, graffiti, or were missing, provide clear photo evidence.

*   **Permit Holder**: If you have a valid permit but it wasn't visible, councils may sometimes waive a first-time oversight.

## How to Submit Your Appeal

1.  **Gather Evidence**: Photos of your car, the signage, and any supporting documents (mechanic receipts, etc.).

2.  **Access the Portal**: Visit the City of Adelaide website and navigate to the 'Request a Review' section.

3.  **Submit Your Statement**: Be clear, concise, and factual. Avoid emotional pleas; stick to the "Lawful Excuse."
