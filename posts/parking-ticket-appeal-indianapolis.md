## Parking Tickets in Indianapolis: What You Need to Know

**Indianapolis is the largest city in Indiana, and its parking enforcement is just as substantial. From Downtown to Broad Ripple, Mass Ave, and around the Lucas Oil Stadium, the City of Indianapolis’ Department of Public Works (DPW) and the Indianapolis Metropolitan Police Department (IMPD) are highly active.**

Parking violations in Indianapolis are civil matters adjudicated through the **Department of Public Works**. The city uses an administrative review and hearing process, which is a formal review of the facts and evidence by a trained hearing officer.

> **Key stat**: Indianapolis has a 30-day window for appeals. While this is longer than some cities, the late fees are aggressive once the deadline passes.

## How Indianapolis Parking Enforcement Works

**Enforcement is handled by the Indianapolis Department of Public Works and the IMPD. Meters are generally enforced Monday through Saturday, from 7:00 AM to 11:00 PM, though some areas vary.**

Common ticket types and fine amounts in Indianapolis:

1. **Expired Meter** — $25

2. **Loading Zone Violation** — $40

3. **No Parking/Standing Zone** — $40

4. **Fire Hydrant** — $100

5. **Bus Zone Violation** — $100

6. **Handicap Space Violation** — $250

7. **Blocking Driveway/Sidewalk** — $40

## The Review and Hearing Process

**If you wish to dispute a ticket, you must request a review. Indianapolis offers several ways to handle this process:**

1. **The Request**: You must request the review within 30 days.

2. **The Review**: A hearing officer reviews the citation and your evidence. This can often be done remotely or via email.

3. **The Evidence**: Indianapolis hearing officers place a high value on photographic evidence and time-stamped receipts from parking apps like **ParkIndy**.

4. **The Appeal**: If you lose at the administrative level, you can appeal to a Marion County Court Judge within 30 days.

## How to Appeal an Indianapolis Parking Ticket

### Step 1: Document Your Case Immediately

**Mass Ave and Downtown Indianapolis have very specific parking rules that can change by the block. If you get a ticket, take photos of where you parked, the nearest signs, and the parking meter.** If you used a parking app, save a screenshot of your active session.

### Step 2: Submit Your Appeal

You must submit your appeal within **30 calendar days** of the ticket date.

- **Online**: Visit indy.gov/parking or ParkIndy.info to upload your appeal.

- **By Phone**: You can call 317-327-3100 to inquire about your options.

- **In Person**: Visit the Department of Public Works at 1200 Madison Ave. (8:00 AM - 5:00 PM).

### Step 3: Present Your Defense

During your hearing, be concise and stick to the facts. If you were ticketed for an expired meter but the meter was broken, providing a photo of the "Out of Order" message or a service report number is your best defense.

## Strongest Grounds for Appeal in Indianapolis

**Indianapolis hearing officers are generally reasonable if you can prove a specific error or a technical failure.**

1. **Broken Meter**: If a meter or kiosk failed to accept payment, provide a photo and a report number from the City’s meter maintenance line.

2. **Inadequate Signage**: If a "No Parking" sign was missing, damaged, or not visible from where you parked, provide photos.

3. **Factual Error**: If the ticket lists the wrong plate, make/model, or location, the citation is technically invalid.

4. **Valid Permit**: If you have a valid residential or disability permit and it was properly displayed, provide proof.

5. **Emergency Situations**: Documented medical emergencies or vehicle breakdowns (with a tow receipt) are often considered valid defenses.

## Indianapolis-Specific Legal Points

**Parking is governed by the Indianapolis Municipal Code (Chapter 621). Key points to remember:**

1. **The 30-Day Rule**: This is the most important rule in Indianapolis. Do not miss this deadline, or your fine will increase and you will lose your right to a hearing.

2. **Broad Ripple Enforcement**: Be extra careful in Broad Ripple on weekend nights. They have specialized enforcement teams focused on congestion and safety.

3. **Event Parking**: During major events at the stadium or arena, parking rates and rules can change. Always check the sign or the app.
