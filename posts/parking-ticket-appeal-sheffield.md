# How to Appeal a Parking Ticket in Sheffield

Sheffield City Council uses a specialized digital portal to handle Penalty Charge Notice (PCN) appeals. If you've been ticketed in the "Steel City," following the correct procedure can save you 50% of the fine or get it cancelled entirely.

## Common Sheffield Parking Hotspots

Certain districts in Sheffield have particularly high rates of enforcement:

- **Devonshire Quarter & West Street**: Popular student and nightlife hubs with very limited parking and strict permit-only zones.

- **The Moor**: The main pedestrianized shopping area has frequent loading bay and "no stopping" enforcement.

- **Kelham Island**: Rapidly developing area where parking zones have recently changed, catching many drivers unaware.

## Key Sheffield Parking Rules

- **PCN Price Levels**: Fines are tiered at **£70** for serious offences (yellow lines, bus lanes) and **£50** for minor ones (expired meters). Paying within 14 days reduces the cost by half.

- **Observation Period**: Sheffield typically allows a **5-minute observation period** for non-loading offences and up to **10-20 minutes** if there is active evidence of commercial loading/unloading.

- **On-Street vs Off-Street**: Be careful in Council-run surface car parks; the rules can differ slightly from street-side parking, especially regarding the use of parking apps vs physical tickets.

## The Appeal Process

1. **Submit Evidence Online**: Use the [Sheffield Taranto Portal](https://sheffield.tarantoportal.com/) to upload photos of missing signs, your parking receipt, or a broken meter.

2. **First Stage Rejection**: If your informal challenge is rejected, the council will offer a further 14 days to pay at the discounted rate before proceeding to the formal "Notice to Owner" stage.

3. **Traffic Penalty Tribunal**: Sheffield PCNs can be appealed to an independent adjudicator if you are unsatisfied with the council's formal response.
